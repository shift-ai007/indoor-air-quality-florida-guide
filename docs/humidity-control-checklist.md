# Humidity Control Checklist for South Florida Homeowners

A practical, room-by-room checklist for maintaining optimal indoor humidity levels in South Florida's subtropical climate.

## Weekly Checks

- [ ] Read hygrometer in main living area — target 45-50% RH
- [ ] Check AC filter for visible dust buildup
- [ ] Verify AC is cycling properly (10-15 min minimum run times)
- [ ] Check condensate drain line is flowing (water dripping outside)
- [ ] Run bathroom exhaust fans during and 20 minutes after showers

## Monthly Checks

- [ ] Check around all supply and return vents for condensation or staining
- [ ] Inspect visible ductwork in garage or attic for moisture, disconnections, or damaged insulation
- [ ] Clean bathroom exhaust fan grilles
- [ ] Check dryer vent for blockage (lint buildup increases indoor humidity)
- [ ] Inspect window seals and weatherstripping for gaps

## Quarterly Actions

- [ ] Replace HVAC air filter (MERV 11-13 recommended)
- [ ] Clean condensate drain with vinegar flush
- [ ] Check attic for signs of moisture intrusion or condensation on ductwork
- [ ] Verify thermostat humidity readings match standalone hygrometer

## Annual Professional Service

- [ ] Full HVAC system inspection and coil cleaning
- [ ] Duct leakage test (target <10% leakage)
- [ ] Refrigerant charge verification
- [ ] Condensate pan and drain inspection
- [ ] Air quality baseline measurement (PM2.5, CO2, humidity, VOCs)

## Room-by-Room Humidity Hotspots

### Kitchen
- **Risk**: Cooking steam, dishwasher, under-sink leaks
- **Fix**: Always run range hood when cooking. Check under-sink connections quarterly.

### Bathrooms
- **Risk**: Shower steam, poor ventilation, grout deterioration
- **Fix**: Exhaust fan must run 20 min post-shower. Re-grout annually. Check fan CFM rating — most Florida bathrooms need 80+ CFM.

### Master Bedroom
- **Risk**: People generate 1-2 pints of moisture per night through breathing and perspiration
- **Fix**: Keep door slightly open for air circulation, or ensure dedicated return air vent.

### Laundry Room
- **Risk**: Washer leaks, dryer steam, standing water
- **Fix**: Verify dryer vents outside (not into attic). Check washer hoses for bulging or cracking.

### Garage (if connected to house)
- **Risk**: Humid air infiltration through door seals, water heater condensation
- **Fix**: Weather-strip the house-to-garage door. Insulate hot water pipes.

### Attic
- **Risk**: Temperature differentials cause condensation on ductwork
- **Fix**: Ensure all duct joints are sealed with mastic. Verify insulation R-value meets Florida code (R-30 minimum).

## Emergency Response: What to Do During a Humidity Spike

1. **Check your AC** — Is it running? Is the fan on AUTO (not ON)?
2. **Check the thermostat** — Set to 75°F or lower during humidity emergencies
3. **Close all windows and doors** — Even if it feels nice outside, the humidity is the enemy
4. **Run all exhaust fans** — Bathroom, kitchen range hood
5. **If humidity stays above 65% for 24+ hours** — Call for [emergency service](https://ac-repair.today/services/emergency-ac-repair/) before mold establishes
